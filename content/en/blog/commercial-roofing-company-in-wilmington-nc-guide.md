Choosing a roofer isn’t something most homeowners do very often—but when you do, it’s a big decision. The right contractor protects your home, your wallet, and your peace of mind. The wrong one can leave you with leaks, damage, and expensive headaches.

Use this Mendoza Checklist before you sign any roofing contract in **Brunswick County** (and the greater Wilmington area).

---

## 1. Confirm They’re Local and Established

A strong local presence is your first line of defense against fly‑by‑night storm chasers.

**What to look for:**

- A physical address in or near **Brunswick County / Wilmington**
- A local phone number you can easily reach
- Evidence of years in business serving your area
- Trucks, yard signs, or references you can actually see around town

**Red flags:**

- Only a P.O. box or out‑of‑state address  
- No history in the community or no online presence  
- They “just arrived after the storm” and are here for a limited time

---

## 2. Verify License and Insurance

You should never feel uncomfortable asking a roofer to prove they’re qualified and protected.

**Ask for:**

- **Contractor license:** Confirm it’s active and in good standing
- **General liability insurance:** Protects your property if something goes wrong
- **Workers’ compensation:** Protects you from liability if a worker is injured on your property

A reputable roofer will gladly email or text you a copy of their certificates before work begins.

---

## 3. Check Reviews, References, and Local Work

Real‑world proof matters more than any sales pitch.

**Your checklist:**

- **Online reviews:** Look at Google, Facebook, and the Better Business Bureau  
- **Photo gallery:** Ask to see before‑and‑after pictures of roofs similar to yours  
- **References:** Get at least 2–3 recent customers in Brunswick County or Wilmington you can call  
- **Drive‑by addresses:** Many good roofers can point you to past projects you can see from the street

When you talk to references, ask:

- Were they on time and on budget?  
- How did they handle clean‑up?  
- Would you hire them again?

---

## 4. Demand a Detailed, Written Estimate

A trustworthy roofer is transparent about price and scope—no “handshake only” deals.

**Your estimate should clearly state:**

- Scope of work (repair vs. full replacement)  
- Type, brand, and color of shingles or roofing system  
- Underlayment, flashing, ventilation, and other components  
- Labor cost and total project price  
- Any potential additional costs (e.g., rotten wood replacement)  
- Estimated start and completion dates

**Avoid:**

- Vague one‑line quotes  
- Pushy sales tactics like “today‑only price”  
- Contractors who won’t put everything in writing

---

## 5. Understand Warranties (Both Material and Labor)

A roof is more than shingles—it’s a system. Each part should be backed by a warranty.

**You want to know:**

- **Manufacturer warranty:** How long are the shingles or materials covered? What’s included (defects, wind, algae)?  
- **Workmanship warranty:** How long does the contractor stand behind their installation?  
- **What voids the warranty:** Poor ventilation, homeowner alterations, unapproved repairs, etc.

If a roofer can’t clearly explain the warranties, that’s a concern.

---

## 6. Ask About Experience With Your Type of Roof

Brunswick County homes and buildings face coastal conditions—wind, rain, heat, and occasional tropical systems.

Make sure the roofer is experienced with:

- **Your roof style:** Steep‑slope shingle, metal, flat, or low‑slope  
- **Your property type:** Residential, commercial, or multifamily  
- **Storm‑related repairs:** Wind and hail damage, emergency tarping, and insurance coordination

The right contractor will explain **why** they recommend a specific material or system for your home, not just “this is what we always use.”

---

## 7. Review Their Safety and Clean‑Up Plan

A professional roofer protects your home during and after the job—not just when it’s finished.

**Ask how they will:**

- Protect landscaping, driveways, and outdoor furniture  
- Handle tear‑off and debris (dumpster location, haul‑away plan)  
- Use magnet sweeps to pick up nails around your yard and driveway  
- Ensure workers follow safety practices on your property

After the job, your property should look **cleaner** than when they arrived—except for the fresh new roof.

---

## 8. Clarify the Payment Schedule

Good roofers don’t need full payment before work starts.

**Typical, safe structure:**

- A reasonable deposit once the contract is signed  
- Progress or final payment after work is completed and you’ve inspected it

**Be cautious if:**

- They demand cash only  
- They pressure you to sign over your entire insurance check  
- There’s no written payment schedule in the contract

---

## 9. Make Sure Communication Feels Right

Roofing can be technical, but your experience shouldn’t be confusing.

Look for a roofer who:

- Listens to your concerns and answers questions clearly  
- Explains the process step‑by‑step  
- Provides a single point of contact (project manager or office staff)  
- Keeps you updated if weather or materials delay the job

If communication is poor **before** the contract is signed, it usually gets worse later.

---

## 10. Confirm They Stand Behind Their Work

The relationship shouldn’t end the day the last shingle goes on.

Ask:

- What happens if there’s a leak after the job?  
- How quickly do they respond to warranty calls or concerns?  
- Do they offer maintenance, inspections, or follow‑up visits?

A roofer who plans to be around for years will be committed to return if something needs attention.

---

## Quick “Mendoza Checklist” Summary

Before you choose a roofer in Brunswick County, confirm:

1. **Local, established company** with a real presence in the area  
2. **Licensed and properly insured**  
3. **Strong local reviews and references**  
4. **Clear, written, detailed estimate**  
5. **Solid material and workmanship warranties**  
6. **Experience with your roof type and coastal conditions**  
7. **Documented safety and clean‑up practices**  
8. **Fair, written payment terms**  
9. **Professional, responsive communication**  
10. **Willingness to stand behind their work long‑term**

Print or save this checklist and use it when comparing contractors. The right roofer will make it easy to check every box.
