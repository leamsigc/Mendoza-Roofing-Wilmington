Choosing a **commercial roofing company in Wilmington, NC** isn't something you do every year—but when you do, it's a major decision. The wrong contractor can leave your building with leaks, code violations, and unplanned downtime that hurts your business. The right one protects your property, your budget, and your peace of mind for decades.

This **Mendoza Checklist** is built specifically for **business owners, property managers, HOAs, facility directors, and investors** across **Wilmington and Brunswick County** who want to hire the right commercial roofing contractor the first time.

Use it to compare quotes, ask better questions, and avoid costly mistakes.

---

## 1. Confirm They're Truly Local & Established in Commercial Roofing

For commercial roofing, a strong **local presence** and **commercial track record** are essential. Coastal North Carolina is not a place for trial-and-error.

**What to look for:**

- A physical address **in or near Wilmington, NC** (not just a P.O. box).
- A **local phone number** answered during normal business hours.
- At least **5-10 years of experience** with commercial roofs in the Wilmington / Brunswick County area.
- A portfolio of **commercial projects** you can actually see—office buildings, shopping centers, warehouses, churches, multi-family, HOAs.

**Red flags:**

- They “just arrived after the storm” and are “here for a limited time.”
- No examples of commercial jobs—only homes.
- Out-of-state plates on trucks, no local reviews, no local references.

> **Mendoza Checklist Item #1:**  
> Is this a **local, established commercial roofer** with visible projects in Wilmington and Brunswick County?

---

## 2. Verify License, Insurance, and Commercial-Grade Coverage

A commercial roof is a big investment. You should never be shy about asking for proof that your contractor is properly licensed and insured.

**Ask for copies of:**

- **North Carolina contractor license** in good standing.
- **Commercial general liability insurance** (ideally $1-$2 million+).
- **Workers' compensation** covering all workers on your roof.
- **Additional insured** documentation if your building or lender requires it.

A reputable commercial roofing contractor will:

- Provide certificates **before** work starts.
- List you or your management company as **certificate holder** if requested.
- Never pressure you into **cash-only** deals or ask you to pay other subcontractors directly.

> **Mendoza Checklist Item #2:**  
> Are licensing and **commercial-grade insurance** documented, current, and easy to verify?

---

## 3. Check Reviews, References, and Local Commercial Work

Online ratings tell part of the story—commercial references tell the rest.

**Your review & reference checklist:**

- **Google, Facebook, BBB**: Look for consistent 4.5+ star ratings and reviews that mention:
  - “Commercial roof”
  - “Flat roof”
  - “Business” or “property manager”
- **Photo gallery**: Ask to see before-and-after photos of:
  - Flat or low-slope roofs  
  - Metal roofs  
  - Shopping centers, offices, industrial buildings, churches, HOAs
- **References**: Request at least **2-3 recent commercial clients** in:
  - Wilmington proper (Downtown, Mayfaire, Market Street, Oleander Drive)
  - Brunswick County (Leland, Shallotte, Southport, etc.)

When you speak with references, ask:

- Were they **on time and on budget**?
- Did they communicate clearly and respond quickly?
- How has the roof held up through **storms and heavy rain**?
- Would you hire them again?

> **Mendoza Checklist Item #3:**  
> Can they provide **recent, local commercial references** you can actually contact?

---

## 4. Demand a Detailed, Written, Commercial-Grade Estimate

A serious commercial roofer will never rely on handshakes and vague one-line quotes.

**Your written estimate should clearly spell out:**

- **Scope of work**  
  - Repair, replacement, restoration, or coating system  
  - Areas of the roof affected (entire roof, sections, penetrations)
- **Roof system**  
  - Type (TPO, EPDM, modified bitumen, metal, coating)  
  - Brand/manufacturer and product line  
  - Membrane thickness (e.g., 60 mil TPO), color, and fastening method
- **Components and details**  
  - Insulation: type and R-value  
  - Flashings: walls, curbs, penetrations (HVAC, vents, pipes)  
  - Edge metal and gutters  
  - Ventilation and drainage improvements
- **Project logistics**  
  - Start date and estimated completion date  
  - How they will stage materials (parking lot, laydown areas)  
  - Daily work hours and how they'll minimize impact on your business
- **Pricing and payment schedule**  
  - Total cost, plus breakdown per square foot or per section if helpful  
  - Clear payment milestones (deposit, progress draws, final payment)  
  - Any allowances for hidden damage (e.g., rotten decking replacement at a set rate)

**Avoid:**

- “We'll figure it out once we get started.”  
- Quotes that only list a **single lump sum** with no explanation.  
- High-pressure tactics like “This price is only good today.”

> **Mendoza Checklist Item #4:**  
> Is the estimate **detailed, transparent, and written** so there are no surprises?

## 5. Understand Warranties: Material + Workmanship

A commercial roof must be backed by strong warranties to protect your investment.

**Key warranty questions:**

- **Manufacturer warranty**  
  - How many years is it good for? (20, 25, 30+ years?)  
  - Is it a **material-only** warranty or a **full system** warranty?  
  - What wind speeds are covered? Is ponding water included or excluded?
- **Workmanship (labor) warranty**  
  - How long does the contractor stand behind their installation—3, 5, 10 years?  
  - What exactly is covered, and how quickly do they respond to warranty calls?
- **What voids the warranty?**  
  - Unapproved penetrations (new HVAC units, satellite dishes).  
  - Neglected maintenance (clogged drains, debris left on roof).  
  - Work by other contractors who cut or damage the membrane.

Ask to see **sample warranty documents** in writing—not just verbal promises.

> **Mendoza Checklist Item #5:**  
> Do you fully understand your **material and workmanship warranties**, in writing?

---

## 6. Ensure Experience with Your Roof Type & Coastal Conditions

Wilmington's coastal climate brings **heat, humidity, salt air, storm winds, and heavy rain**. Your roofer needs to understand how that affects commercial roofs.

**Ask about their experience with:**

- **Roof types**  
  - Flat and low-slope TPO, EPDM, PVC  
  - Metal standing-seam and R-panel  
  - Modified bitumen and BUR  
  - Roof coating systems for extending an existing roof's life
- **Coastal challenges**  
  - Wind uplift along edges and corners  
  - Ponding water and drainage design  
  - Corrosion of metal components from salt air  
  - Fastener choice and securement for high-wind events
- **Your specific building use**  
  - Medical facilities, cold storage, food service, data centers, churches, schools, HOAs—each has different priorities and code considerations.

A knowledgeable contractor will explain **why** they recommend a specific system for your property, not just “this is what we always do.”

> **Mendoza Checklist Item #6:**  
> Do they have clear, specific experience with **your roof type and coastal conditions**?

---

## 7. Review Their Safety Plan, Clean-Up, and Business Disruption Strategy

A commercial roofing project shouldn't feel like chaos.

**Ask how they will:**

- **Protect people and property**  
  - Safety barriers, cones, and caution tape  
  - Keeping entrances, walkways, and emergency exits clear  
  - Coordinating with your team on deliveries, trash pickup, and customer flow
- **Manage debris and nails**  
  - Where the dumpster or dump trailer will go  
  - How often they haul debris offsite  
  - How many times per day they perform **magnetic nail sweeps**
- **Minimize business disruption**  
  - Planning noisy work during off-hours where possible  
  - Protecting condensers, rooftop units, signage, and skylights  
  - Working in phases so tenants or customers continue operating safely

After the job, your property should look **cleaner** than when they arrived—except for the new roof.

> **Mendoza Checklist Item #7:**  
> Is there a clear **safety and clean-up plan** that respects your staff, tenants, and customers?

---

## 8. Clarify Payment Schedule, Terms, and Financing

You should never feel rushed or unsure about how and when you're paying.

**Look for:**

- A **reasonable deposit** (commonly 10-30%, depending on project size and materials).  
- **Progress payments** tied to clear milestones (e.g., after tear-off, after membrane installation, after final inspection).  
- Final payment only after your **walk-through** and punch-list items are completed.

**Be cautious of:**

- Demands for large **up-front payments** before materials are even ordered.  
- Cash-only discounts that leave you without a paper trail.  
- Contractors asking you to sign over your entire insurance check.

If needed, ask if they offer **financing options** or can structure the project in phases to align with your budget or capital improvement plan.

> **Mendoza Checklist Item #8:**  
> Are the **payment terms fair, written, and easy to understand**?

---

## 9. Evaluate Communication and Project Management

A commercial roofing project can be complex. Clear communication keeps everyone aligned.

**Expect from a professional commercial roofer:**

- A **single point of contact** (project manager, superintendent, or account manager).  
- A clear **project schedule** with milestones and expected completion dates.  
- Regular updates via **email, text, or portal** on:  
  - Weather delays  
  - Material delivery changes  
  - Inspection results  
  - Punch-list progress

Pay attention to how they communicate **before** you sign:

- Do they return calls promptly?  
- Are their estimates and emails professional and clear?  
- Do they answer technical questions patiently?

Poor communication at the start usually gets worse later.

> **Mendoza Checklist Item #9:**  
> Is their **communication professional, proactive, and easy to work with**?

---

## 10. Confirm They Offer Long-Term Support and Maintenance

Commercial roofs last longest when they're maintained properly.

**Ask about:**

- **Preventative maintenance programs**  
  - Annual or semi-annual inspections  
  - Cleaning debris and drains  
  - Minor repairs before they become major leaks
- **Documentation**  
  - Photo reports and written findings after each inspection  
  - Recommendations prioritized by urgency and budget
- **Post-project support**  
  - How quickly do they respond if there's a leak?  
  - Do they offer discounted service rates for roofs they installed?  
  - Will they help with insurance documentation after a storm?

The relationship shouldn't end the day the last fastener is driven or the last membrane seam is welded.

> **Mendoza Checklist Item #10:**  
> Do they offer a **clear plan to maintain and support your roof long-term**?

## Commercial Roof System Comparison for Wilmington, NC

Choosing the right system is half the battle. Here's how common commercial systems compare in our climate.

| Roof System | Typical Lifespan | Best For | Approx. Cost (2024) | Key Benefits in Wilmington |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **TPO (Thermoplastic Polyolefin)** | 20-30 years | Flat/low-slope roofs, warehouses, retail centers | ~$8-$12/ft² | Highly reflective (lower cooling costs), strong welded seams, good for ponding-prone areas with proper design |
| **EPDM (Rubber)** | 25-35 years | Flat roofs with rooftop equipment, retrofits | ~$6-$10/ft² | Flexible, proven long-term performance, easy to patch |
| **Modified Bitumen** | 15-25 years | Smaller/commercial roofs, walkable areas | ~$7-$10/ft² | Durable, layered system, handles foot traffic well |
| **Metal (Standing-Seam)** | 40-50+ years | Low-slope and pitched commercial roofs, high-visibility buildings | ~$12-$18/ft² | Excellent wind resistance, very long lifespan, can be highly reflective |
| **Roof Coatings (Silicone, Acrylic, Polyurea)** | 10-20 years (can be re-coated) | Extending life of existing roof, budget-friendly upgrades | ~$5-$9/ft² | Seals small leaks, adds UV protection, minimal tear-off and disruption |

> These ranges are **general estimates**. Actual pricing depends on roof size, condition, access, and specific manufacturer.

## Repair vs. Replace: A Practical Decision Guide

Sometimes you don't need a new roof—sometimes you do. Use these guidelines when reviewing proposals.

| Roof Condition | Likely Best Option | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Roof under 10 years, isolated leaks, good drainage | **Targeted repair** | Cost-effective; preserves existing system |
| 10-20+ years, widespread cracks, bubbles, multiple leaks | **Partial or full replacement** | Repairs will be frequent and add up quickly |
| Severe ponding water, saturated insulation, recurring leaks | **Full replacement with improved drainage** | Fixing symptoms without solving drainage is a short-term patch |
| Hail or wind storm damage, insurance involvement | **Emergency repair + replacement** (as needed) | Protects building immediately, then allows proper claim handling |
| Selling or refinancing property soon | **Roof tune-up or replacement (case-by-case)** | A healthy roof can increase value and buyer confidence |

A trustworthy commercial roofer will:

- Explain **both** options when they're viable.  
- Show you photos and, when possible, cores or moisture scans, so you see what they see.  
- Help you understand the **long-term cost** of patching vs. replacing.

## When You Should Call for Emergency Commercial Roof Service

Don't wait if you notice:

- Active leaks near **electrical equipment or data rooms**.  
- Water dripping in **public areas** (lobbies, hallways, stairwells).  
- New stains or bubbling after severe **storms or hurricanes**.  
- Fast-spreading damage around rooftop units.

Most reputable commercial roofers in Wilmington offer **24/7 emergency response** for tarping, temporary repairs, and leak mitigation.
