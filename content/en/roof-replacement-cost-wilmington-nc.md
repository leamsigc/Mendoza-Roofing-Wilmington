## What Affects Your Roof Replacement Cost in Wilmington?

### Roof Size and Pitch
Larger roofs cost more — that's obvious. But **steep roofs** cost significantly more because they require additional safety equipment, more labor time, and specialized installation techniques. A 2-story home with a steep pitch can cost 20-40% more than a single-story ranch with the same square footage.

### Number of Existing Layers
If your home has **two or more layers** of old roofing, the tear-off cost increases. Each additional layer adds $1,000-$3,000 to the project. Most Wilmington homes have 1-2 layers.

### Decking Condition
When we remove your old roof, we inspect the wood decking underneath. **Rotted or damaged decking** requires replacement — typically $500-$2,000 depending on extent. This is impossible to know until the old roof is removed, which is why written estimates include a decking allowance.

### Coastal Building Code Requirements
Wilmington and Brunswick County have **stricter building codes** than inland NC:
- Wind-rated shingles (130+ mph minimum) — adds $500-$1,500
- Enhanced fastening patterns — adds $300-$800
- Ice and water shield at eaves and valleys — adds $400-$800
- Corrosion-resistant flashing and fasteners — adds $200-$500

These aren't optional — they're required by code. Any contractor quoting significantly below these ranges may be cutting corners.

### Material Choice
This is the biggest cost driver. Here's the tradeoff:

**Asphalt shingles** = lowest upfront cost, shortest lifespan. You'll replace them 2-3 times over the life of a metal roof.

**Metal roofing** = 2-3x the upfront cost, but lasts 2-3x longer. Over 50 years, metal often costs less per year.

**Tile roofing** = premium cost, premium lifespan. Best for high-end homes where aesthetics matter.

## Roof Replacement Cost vs. Repair Cost

Not every roof needs replacement. Here's when repair makes sense vs. replacement:

| Situation | Recommended Action | Estimated Cost |
|-----------|-------------------|---------------|
| 1-3 missing shingles | Repair | $150 – $400 |
| Small leak in one area | Repair | $400 – $900 |

| Widespread shingle damage | **Replace** | $12,000 – $18,000+ |
