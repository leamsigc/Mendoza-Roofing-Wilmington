## What Is a FORTIFIED Roof?

The FORTIFIED Roof program was developed by the **Insurance Institute for Business & Home Safety (IBHS)** — the research arm of the property insurance industry. It's not a brand or a material. It's a set of **engineering-backed construction standards** designed to make homes significantly more resistant to hurricane damage.

A FORTIFIED Roof requires three critical upgrades beyond standard building code:

1. **Enhanced Roof Deck Attachment** — Ring-shank nails (not standard smooth nails) at closer spacing to prevent deck uplift in high winds
2. **Improved Water Barrier Protection** — Sealed roof deck with self-adhering underlayment at eaves, valleys, and penetrations
3. **Reinforced Roof-to-Wall Connections** — Enhanced connections between the roof structure and walls to prevent the roof from lifting off

These upgrades are tested and proven. IBHS research shows that FORTIFIED Roofs suffer **significantly less damage** in hurricanes compared to code-built roofs.

## FORTIFIED Roof Materials We Install

### Standing Seam Metal Roofing (Best for FORTIFIED)
Standing seam metal is the **top choice for FORTIFIED certification** in coastal NC:
- Concealed clip fastening system resists wind uplift far better than nailed materials
- Interlocking panels have no individual pieces to blow off
- Easily exceeds 140+ mph wind requirements
- 40-70 year lifespan
- Ideal for homes within 1 mile of the coast

### Architectural Asphalt Shingles (FORTIFIED-Compliant)
Architectural shingles can qualify for FORTIFIED certification when installed with enhanced methods:
- Ring-shank nails at 4-6 inch spacing (vs. standard 6-inch)
- Sealed roof deck with self-adhering underlayment
- Enhanced ridge and hip shingle adhesion
- Class 4 impact-resistant shingles available for hail protection
- Best value option for most Wilmington homes

### Impact-Resistant Shingles
For homes in hail-prone areas of Brunswick County:
- UL 2218 Class 4 impact rating (highest available)
- Withstands golf ball-sized hail without damage
- Meets FORTIFIED impact resistance requirements
- Often qualifies for additional insurance discounts

## The FORTIFIED Roof Installation Process

### Step 1: FORTIFIED Evaluator Assessment
A **certified FORTIFIED Evaluator** inspects your existing roof structure to determine what upgrades are needed. This includes checking deck attachment, current underlayment, and roof-to-wall connections.

### Step 2: Custom Upgrade Plan
Based on the evaluation, we create a plan that meets FORTIFIED standards for your specific home. Not every home needs the same upgrades — the evaluator identifies exactly what's required.

### Step 3: Professional Installation
Our crews install the FORTIFIED upgrades following IBHS specifications:
- Replace smooth nails with ring-shank nails at required spacing
- Install self-adhering underlayment at all critical areas
- Seal roof edges, valleys, and penetrations
- Reinforce roof-to-wall connections as needed
- Install chosen roofing material (metal, architectural shingles, or impact-resistant)

### Step 4: Evaluator Inspection & Documentation
The FORTIFIED Evaluator returns to inspect the completed installation. They document every step with photos and measurements.

### Step 5: IBHS Certification
The evaluator submits documentation to IBHS for review. Upon approval, you receive your **FORTIFIED Roof designation certificate** — valid for 5 years and eligible for insurance discounts.
